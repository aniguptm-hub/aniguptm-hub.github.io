Expense Manager. Learning by building

I am building a personal expense manager to understand how financial data can be parsed, categorized, and corrected automatically without relying on bank APIs.

The problem

Most expense trackers depend on delayed bank sync or manual entry. Email and notification data exists in real time, but it is noisy, inconsistent, and easy to misclassify.

What I am building

  • Email ingestion to capture transaction-related messages
  • Parsing logic to extract amount, merchant, and transaction type
  • Basic categorization with room for manual correction

What I am learning

  • Why financial data is messy even when it looks structured
  • How small edge cases break naive automation
  • Why user overrides are as important as automatic rules

Notes and learnings

  • UPI Lite looks like a wallet but behaves very differently underneath
  • Why “payment successful” does not always mean money settled
